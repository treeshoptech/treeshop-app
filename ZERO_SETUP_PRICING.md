## Smart Defaults Strategy

### Why 45% Target Margin?

**45% is the tree care industry standard** for:
- ‚úÖ Covers unexpected costs and complexity
- ‚úÖ Accounts for non-billable time
- ‚úÖ Provides buffer for AFISS adjustments
- ‚úÖ Competitive but protects profitability
- ‚úÖ Room for discounts without losing money

**Formula Used:**
```
Billing Rate = Cost √∑ (1 - Margin%)
$450 = $247.50 √∑ (1 - 0.45)
$450 = $247.50 √∑ 0.55
```

**NOT:** `Cost √ó (1 + Markup%)` ‚Üê This gives wrong margin!

## AFISS: The Competitive Moat

**Access, Facilities, Irregularities, Site, Safety**

35+ complexity factors automatically adjust pricing:
- Narrow gate access: +12%

## FAQ

### Q: Can I edit service templates?
**A:** Yes! Service templates are **starting points**. Adjust them based on:
- Your actual equipment costs
- Your labor rates and crew efficiency
- Your local market rates
- Your desired profit margins

### Q: Will my pricing be accurate without loadouts?
**A:** Service templates provide **industry-average** pricing that's:
- Conservative (protects your margins)
- Competitive (based on real market data)
- Adjustable (AFISS factors add site-specific complexity)

For most tree service companies, these starting values are within 10-15% of actual costs. As you create loadouts and complete jobs, your estimates become increasingly precise.

### Q: What if my costs are way different from the defaults?
**A:** Three options:
1. **Quick:** Adjust service template billing rates in Settings
2. **Better:** Create a loadout with your actual equipment/crew costs
3. **Best:** Complete a few jobs, let the system learn your actual PPH and costs

The system is designed to **learn and improve** over time.

### Q: Do I lose money if I price with templates instead of loadouts?
**A:** No! Service templates use **conservative** industry averages:
- 45% target margin (high for tree care)
- Proven PPH rates from established companies
- AFISS adds 10-50% to base pricing
- Locked pricing protects you from cost overruns

Most companies find template pricing is actually **higher** than their gut-feel estimates, which means better margins.

### Q: When should I create my first loadout?
**A:** After your first 3-5 completed jobs. By then you'll know:
- Your actual equipment hourly costs
- Your crew's real production rate (PPH)
- How your costs compare to service templates
- Which services are most profitable

Don't rush loadout creation. Service templates work great for proposals.

## Technical Notes

### Auto-Seeding Behavior
- Runs on first visit to **Settings ‚Üí Line Items** page
- Idempotent (safe to run multiple times)
- Only creates templates that don't exist
- Won't overwrite your custom adjustments
- Also seeds AFISS factors (35 default factors)

### Data Storage
- Service templates: Organization-scoped (your company's standards)
- AFISS factors: System-wide + custom org factors
- Proposals: Locked at creation (preserves pricing)
- Work orders: Links to loadout (tracks actual costs)

### Pricing Formula Consistency
All calculators use the same pattern:
```javascript
// 1. Calculate work volume (service-specific formula)
baseScore = calculateWorkVolume(jobDetails)

// 2. Apply AFISS complexity multiplier
adjustedScore = baseScore √ó afissMultiplier

// 3. Convert to hours using service template PPH
estimatedHours = adjustedScore √∑ serviceTemplate.standardPPH

// 4. Calculate price using service template billing rate
clientPrice = estimatedHours √ó serviceTemplate.standardBillingRate

// 5. Lock price when proposal is created ‚úì
```
